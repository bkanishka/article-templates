வாழ்த்துரை

தமிழ்மொழி மற்றும் இலக்கியத்திற்காக எமது தமிழ்மொழி மற்றும் இலக்கிய பன்னாட்டு ஆய்விதழ் (Ijtlls) தம் இலக்கிய மற்றும் ஆராய்ச்சி அரும்பணியைச் செம்மையாக செய்து வருகின்றது. யு.ஜி.சி. கேர் (UGC CARE), எம்.எல்.ஏ (MLA) மற்றும் டி.ஓ.ஏ.ஜே (DOAJ) போன்ற ஆய்வுத் தளங்களில் மதிப்பீடு செய்யப்பட்ட எமது ஆய்விதழில் கௌரவ சிறப்பிதழ் ஆசிரியர்களாகப் பணியாற்றிட தன்னார்வம் கொண்ட பேராசிரியர்களுக்கு வாய்ப்பளித்துள்ளது. இது தமிழ் ஆய்வுலகில் மிகச்சிறந்த பணியாகும். தமிழ்மொழியின் வளர்ச்சியினை எண்ணி செயல்பட்டு தற்கால இலக்கியம்/Modern Literature என்ற பொருண்மையில் சிறப்பிதழ் நடத்தி தரமான ஆய்வுக்கட்டுரைகளைப் பெற்று எமது ஆய்விதழ் வாயிலாக சிறப்பிதழில் பதிப்பித்தமைக்கு அகமகிழ்வடைகிறேன். மேலும், சிறப்பிதழ் ஆசிரியர்களான முனைவர் வீ.ச. அசோக் குமார், முனைவர் மா. முரளி, முனைவர் ஞா. அந்தோணிசுரேஷ், முனைவர் சு. அய்யனார் மற்றும் முனைவர் செ.சாந்தி அவர்களுக்கும் எம்மோடு இச்சிறப்பிதழ் பணியில் ஈடுபட அனுமதி அளித்த வி.இ.நா.செந்திக்குமார நாடார் கல்லூரி, விருதுநகர், காமராஜ் கல்லூரி, தூத்துக்குடி, சதக்கத்துல்லாஹ் அப்பா கல்லூரி, திருநெல்வேலி, பிஷப் ஹீபர் கல்லூரி, திருச்சிராப்பள்ளி, அழகப்பா பல்கலைக்கழகம், காரைக்குடி நிர்வாகத்தினருக்கும் எமது இதழ் சார்பாக நன்றியினை உரித்தாக்குகின்றேன். தமிழ்த்துறைப் பேராசிரியார்கள் அனைவருக்கும் எமது நன்றியினைத் தெரிவித்துக் கொள்கிறேன். தற்கால இலக்கியம் எனும் தலைப்பில் நவீன இலக்கிய உத்திகளைப் பயன்படத்தி எழுதப்பட்ட ஆய்வுக் கட்டுரைகளை தமிழ் ஆய்வுலகிற்குக் கொண்டுவர எத்தணித்தே இச்சிறப்பிதழ் நடத்தப்பெற்றது. அதற்கேற்றவாறு அறிவுத் தெளிவு கொண்ட ஆய்வுக்கட்டுரைகள் பெறப்பட்டு பதிப்பித்ததில் பெருமகிழ்ச்சி அடைகிறேன். ஆய்வுக்கட்டுரைகள் வழங்கி சிறப்பித்தத் தமிழ்ப்பேராசிரியர்கள், ஏனைய அனைத்து துறைப்பேராசிரியர்கள், எழுத்தாளர்கள், தமிழ் ஆர்வலர்கள் மற்றும் ஆராய்ச்சி மாணவர்களை மனதாரப் பாராட்டுகிறோம். தமிழ்மொழி மற்றும் இலக்கிய பன்னாட்டு ஆய்விதழ் செம்மையாய் செயல்பட்டு ஆய்வுக் கட்டுரைகளை இணையத்தில் பதிப்பு செய்து வருகின்றது. எதிர்காலத்தில் தமிழ் ஆராய்ச்சிகளை உலக அரங்கத்திற்கு எடுத்துச் செல்ல ஓர் ஆய்வுக்களமாக எமது இதழ் முன்மாதிரியாகச் செயல்பட்டு வருகிறது. அதற்கு உறுதுணை அளித்த அனைவருக்கும் நன்றி.

1. அ.முத்துலிங்கம் சிறுகதைகளில் அயலகப் பண்பாடுகள்

Diasporic Culture in A.Muthulingam’s Short Stories

S. Abirami & Dr V. Manikandan

Abstract

Culture is a term associated with people, their tradition and related ideologies.  Culture generally refers to the activity and organization that show the privileges and importance to cultural and traditional symbols of a society. Diasporic related cultural conflict are creating identity crisis in the lives of the settlers in the foreign countries for jobs and as refugees. Tamil people have settled as diasporas in many foreign countries. The article is planned to  cull down the history, tendencies, characteristics, insights, ways of life, social structures of Tamil people settled in abroad found in the stories of the tamil writer A.Muthulingam. This crisis also includes language, food, music, religious beliefs, professions and tools too in the life of the migrated people. Hence, this article is entitled as “Diasporic Culture in A.Muthulingam’s Short Stories” to bring out the aforesaid themes from his stories.

Keywords: Diasporic Culture, A.Muthulingam, Short Stories, Language.

2. புதுமைப்பித்தன் சிறுகதைகளில் மிதவாதப் பெண்ணியம்

Moderate Feminism in the Short Stories of Puthumaipittan

A. Archana & Dr V. Manikannan

Abstract

Feminism is the principle by which society recognizes a woman as a living being and encourages her to be given the same rights and freedoms as men. Women are the fundamental cause of the origin of the human race and its various functions in society. Their role from birth to death is significant.  From ancient times the women have said a lot about women who are a part of men’s lives. However, the primary objective of feminism is to achieve the level of equality for men and women in the economy where women have the freedom they need, the ability to act on their own, the ability to break the barriers of slavery built by society.  Therefore, this research paper is titled “Moderate Feminism in the Short Stories of Puthumaipittan” and explores the primary purposes of feminism through the short stories based on chastity, slavery, lust and dowry.

Keywords: Feminism, Short stories, Puthumaipiththan, Dowry abuse.

Migration and Refugee Life of the Eelam Tamils with Reference to the Novels

K. Kavitha & Dr S. Pandima Devi

Abstract

There is a tragic conflict between the Tamil minority and the Sinhala ethnic group in Sri Lanka and has continued in different forms of liberation struggle over the past forty years. This migration has taken place overseas from Sri Lanka to various countries like India, European nations and Australia. The Tamil people as diasporas and refugees has endured lots of critical crisis in their lives. The writers register such events of the people in their creations and portray the voyages, experiences and misery that occurred during this migration. This article seeks to explore the maritime diaspora experiences and challenges the Eelam Tamils face as refugees in the novels Kalinga, Survival, and Ethel.

6. சென்ரியு கவிதைகளின் பொருண்மைகள்

வீ. ஞான செளந்தரி & முனைவர் வே. மணிகண்டன்

Concepts of Senriyu Poems

V. Gnana Soundari & Dr V. Manikandan

Abstract

Senriyu poems are created on the basis of humanity. Senriyu is a new literary form derived from Haiku. Senriyu has a tendency to act independently of the restrictions of Haiku such as nature, zen principles, lofty style, and goal. Although Haiku and Senriyu are three-dimensional poems, they are conceptually different. Senriyu poetry is distinguished from other poetic literatures by the expressive nature of Senriyu langue related to mankind, which is created by words used by man in practice. Senriyu terms include truth telling, composition, humor, humor, narration, and motto are discussed in detail in this article.Hence, this article decodes the concepts of the Senriyu genre in Tamil.

Keywords: Senriyu, Humanity, Humor, Amusement, Riddle.

The Creative Background of Anton Chekhov

Dr P.Thamilarasi

Abstract

Chekhov is a well-known writer in the Tamil community and world literature in Tamil translation. His ‘Chameleon’ story is very popular. Chekhov's life is as tragic as his stories. Loneliness is the main theme in all his stories. In addition, he has meticulously written about how women deal with loneliness of life. The focus of his stories is as subtle as how the family is tasteless, power struggle, hypocrisy of the rich and the ignorance of the doctors. Chekhov has made excellent use of the conscious technique in his stories. His is simple without stylized language. In thinking about alternative education, he also ran a school to implement it but not successful. Modern Tamil short stories were created under the influence of Russian literature. Chekhov has an essential role to play in that. This article aims to explore Chekhov's creative background.

Keywords: Anton Chekhov, Grounds for creation, Loneliness.

The Life Environment of the Fishermen Community in Kadalukkulle… Kadalukkulle

Dr N. NishaNavis

Abstract

The fishermen community (Neithal People) of the Tamil land are a native set of people with a long historical tradition and cultural significance. These people are world famous for pearl trade in Tamil Nadu. One upon a time, they had business relations with many foreign countries. One of the sangam poems is about heroism and love of the fisherman community. In the middle Ages, these people fell for a number of reasons. The continuation of that fall is still in people’s lives. Today, the problems faced by fishermen community are poverty, inequal economy, inequality, marine risks and problems. They face untold miseries. The fishermen society endures illiteracy, violence and women status continues to be very poor and pathetic. child labor status is also prevalent. These situations have been complied into a short story anthology based on the experiences of the short story writer C. Berlin. This article delineates the chose themes of the study.

Keywords: Meenavan, Kudumba Soolal, Varumai Nilai, Kalviinmai, Thozhil Porattam.

Saraswathi in Andal Priyadarshini’s Short Stories

முனைவர் ஜோ. பென்னி

Marxist Thoughts in the Poems of Jeeva

Dr J. Benny

Abstract

Workers are the backbone of the society, just as the plowman is the backbone of the world. As the number of businesses in India grew, so did the number of unions that sought to secure workers' security, such as job security, pay rises, medical care, provident funds, and incentives. In addition, a number of Marxist leaders appeared to guide the workers. As far as Tamil Nadu is concerned, Jeeva has a lot more to be proud of than the Commonwealth Party and the trade unions who have done their heartfelt service and alleviated the suffering of the workers. Thus he did not consider selfishness and the whole first cause is humanity and was deeply rooted in him to work for the welfare of the workers. In those days workers, lived in a miserable condition where they had to work as slaves to their employers, earning low wages, without even having specific working hours. Jeeva was the one who changed that and brought many changes and stages of development in the lives of the workers. He was well-acquainted with all, regardless of caste, creed or color, and had the nature to embrace all with brotherly affection. Jeeva can be found wherever workers strike. Labor leader Jeeva was called because he worked for the progress of the workers to that extent. This article decodes the Marxian ideas in the poems of Jeeva.

Keywords: Jeeva Kavithaigal, Thozhilaalar, Varumainilai, Adimai Vazhvu, Ontrupaduthal

Creative Compilation Efforts in online Tamil Magazines

Abstract

Internet Tamil is another evolution of the words of Tamil literature. Advances in technology have taken the development of the media to the next level. Olai chuvadi (Paml leaflets) turned into pages and today it has grown to internet Tamil. As far as the internet is concerned, Internet users living in many parts of the world have the power to access online Tamil magazines easily. Most of the online magazines focus on creativity. This article explores the stages in which creative compilation efforts are compiled in online journals through the study of the online journals Thinnai, Pathivukal, Nilasaaral and Vaarppu.
